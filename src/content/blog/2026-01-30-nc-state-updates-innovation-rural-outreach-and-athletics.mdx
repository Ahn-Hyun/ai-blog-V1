# Innovation, Outreach, and Athletics: A Comprehensive Update on NC State

**NC State** University is currently navigating a period of significant activity, marked by strategic advancements in high-tech research, targeted economic outreach to rural communities, and competitive success in collegiate athletics. As the 2024-2025 fiscal year progresses, the institution is leveraging its status as a land-grant university to bridge the gap between elite academic innovation and statewide workforce development. From leading national semiconductor hubs to securing victories on the basketball court, the university's recent initiatives reflect a multi-faceted approach to institutional growth.

## Leadership in Semiconductor and Nuclear Innovation

In the realm of advanced technology, North Carolina State University solidified its standing in 2024 by assuming the leadership role for the CLAWS hub. This designation positions the university at the forefront of national efforts to improve microelectronics capabilities and workforce development. According to the Office of Research and Innovation, this achievement rests on a history of research excellence, including the contributions of Jay Baliga and the university's tenure as the headquarters for the PowerAmerica Institute [ORI Timeline](https://research.ncsu.edu/ori-timeline/).

This academic leadership coincides with major industrial expansion in the surrounding region. Wolfspeed is currently constructing a facility in Chatham County, near the university campus. This development highlights a strategic synergy between the university's research output and the burgeoning high-tech manufacturing ecosystem within North Carolina [ORI Timeline](https://research.ncsu.edu/ori-timeline/).

Parallel to its semiconductor efforts, the university continues to maintain a dominant position in nuclear engineering. Having launched the nation's first nuclear engineering program, the department continues to serve as a premier institution in the field. To support future capabilities, a state budget allocation of $3 million was designated in July 2024 for a nuclear reactor feasibility study. This funding allows the university to evaluate potential next-generation reactor facilities, ensuring the program remains competitive [ORI Timeline](https://research.ncsu.edu/ori-timeline/).

## Expanding Rural Economic Access

While solidifying its high-tech credentials, the university has simultaneously mobilized financial resources to support rural economic stability. For the fiscal year beginning July 1, 2024, the NC State University Foundation awarded 10 grants totaling $427,003 to various colleges and units. These funds are explicitly designated for projects that benefit residents in rural areas of North Carolina [Grant Funding Enables NC State to Reach More Rural Communities](https://news.giving.ncsu.edu/2025/02/grant-funding-enables-nc-state-to-reach-more-rural-communities/).

A primary beneficiary of this funding is the Poole College of Management, which is utilizing the grant to support the Business HIRE program. Scheduled to begin in the summer, this initiative connects university talent with professional roles in rural communities that often face challenges attracting emerging business professionals. The program will facilitate full-time, 12-week paid internships for 11 students, placing them in various rural counties across the state [Grant Funding Enables NC State to Reach More Rural Communities](https://news.giving.ncsu.edu/2025/02/grant-funding-enables-nc-state-to-reach-more-rural-communities/).

### Subsidized Internship Compensation Model

To ensure these internships are financially viable for both students and rural employers, the Business HIRE program employs a subsidized compensation structure supported by the foundation's grant. Interns will receive a total compensation package of $12,000 for the duration of their service.

The funding model is broken down as follows:
*   **Employer Contribution:** The hiring employer is responsible for a base rate of $15 per hour, totaling $7,200 over the course of the internship.
*   **Grant Subsidy:** The foundation's grant provides an additional $10 per hour, adding $4,800 to the student's pay.

### STEM Education Outreach

Beyond business initiatives, the foundation has directed funds toward educational outreach through the College of Education's FLIP-STEM program. The 2024-25 grant supports Saturday workshops designed to engage middle and high school students in complex topics such as design justice and data science. By funding these specialized learning opportunities, the university aims to broaden access to STEM resources for younger students in rural demographics [Grant Funding Enables NC State to Reach More Rural Communities](https://news.giving.ncsu.edu/2025/02/grant-funding-enables-nc-state-to-reach-more-rural-communities/).

## Recent Athletic Performance

Complementing its academic and economic initiatives, the university's athletic program has demonstrated strong form in recent conference play. The men's basketball team secured a notable victory against Syracuse, a win that improved the Wolfpack's standing in the Atlantic Coast Conference (ACC).

The result moved the team's conference record to 6-2, reflecting a game controlled in "convincing fashion."
