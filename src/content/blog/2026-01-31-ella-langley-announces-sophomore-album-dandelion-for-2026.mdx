Country music artist **Ella Langley** enters the next phase of her recording career with the announcement of her sophomore studio album, *Dandelion*. Scheduled for release on April 10, 2026, the project follows a period of commercial growth for the Alabama native, marking an evolution from her debut era [AL.com](https://www.al.com/life/2026/01/alabama-country-star-announces-hotly-awaited-follow-up-to-her-debut-album.html), [American Songwriter](https://americansongwriter.com/ella-langley-celebrates-small-town-freedom-on-new-song-dandelion/).

Building on the foundation of her 2024 debut album, *Hungover*, and the "Still Hungover" tour, *Dandelion* reportedly features an extensive 18-song tracklist. As Langley prepares to join major arena tours and festivals throughout 2026, the industry watches her transition from viral sensation to mainstream figure [AL.com](https://www.al.com/life/2026/01/alabama-country-star-announces-hotly-awaited-follow-up-to-her-debut-album.html).

### From *Hungover* to *Dandelion*
Langley's mainstream breakthrough centered on her 2024 debut *Hungover* and the award-winning duet "You Look Like You Love Me" with Riley Green. This success established her reputation and fueled her 2025 "Still Hungover" tour [AL.com](https://www.al.com/life/2026/01/alabama-country-star-announces-hotly-awaited-follow-up-to-her-debut-album.html).

The turnaround between her 2025 tour and the April 2026 release highlights an aggressive development phase. While *Hungover* relied on collaborative success, the lead-up to *Dandelion* sees Langley achieving solo chart placement with "Choosin' Texas" [AL.com](https://www.al.com/life/2026/01/alabama-country-star-announces-hotly-awaited-follow-up-to-her-debut-album.html).
