The calendar reads February 12, 2026, and the tension in the Spanish capital is palpable. Tonight, the Metropolitano Stadium transforms into a cauldron of noise and pressure as the **Barcelona vs Atltico Madrid** rivalry writes its next chapter in the Copa del Rey semifinals. ### TL;DR

*   **The Matchup:** First leg of the Copa del Rey Semifinals, played at the Metropolitano.

*   **Atletico's Status:** Strong home form with a nearly full-strength attack led by Griezmann and Alvarez.

### The Tactical Landscape: Fortress Metropolitano

Diego Simeone has spent over a decade crafting Atletico Madrid into a side that thrives on suffering, but the 2026 iteration of 'Los Colchoneros' possesses a sharper offensive edge than previous vintages. The integration of Julian Alvarez alongside the evergreen Antoine Griezmann has provided Simeone with a dynamic front line capable of pressing high or punishing teams on the break. According to recent reports, Simeone is expected to deploy a formation that shifts fluidly between a 5-3-2 in defense and a 3-5-2 in attack, utilizing the width of Nahuel Molina and Reinildo or Javi Galan to stretch Barcelona's patchwork defense [SI](https://www.si.com/es-us/futbol/alineaciones-atletico-de-madrid-barcelona-12-2-2026).

Conversely, Barcelona arrives in Madrid with a clear but risky blueprint. Hansi Flick's insistence on a high defensive line has revitalized the Catalans' attack, yet it leaves them vulnerable against teams with precise vertical passing. The absence of Andreas Christensen, who is sidelined with an ACL injury, removes a crucial stabilizer from the backline. This forces young Pau Cubarsi to shoulder immense responsibility alongside Inigo Martinez or Ronald Araujo, creating a 'trial by fire' scenario against the physical presence of Alexander Sorloth and the guile of Griezmann.

### Barcelona's Personnel Crisis: A Test of Depth

The narrative surrounding this semifinal is undeniably shaped by the medical room. Barcelona's injury list reads like a roll call of their most energetic talents. The absence of Gavi is particularly damaging in a fixture that demands high intensity and physical duels. Without his disruptive presence in midfield, Pedri and Frenkie de Jong (or Marc Casado) must control the tempo without getting overrun by Atletico's physical engine room, likely marshaled by Rodrigo De Paul and Koke.

Furthermore, the defensive flanks are a major concern. With Alejandro Balde out, Hector Fort is expected to step in. While talented, Fort faces the daunting task of containing Nahuel Molina's overlapping runs. Reports indicate that Flick has limited options to rotate, forcing him to rely on the chemistry between Lamine Yamal and Robert Lewandowski to outscore any defensive lapses [AS](https://as.com/futbol/alineacion-posible-del-barcelona-ante-al-atletico-hoy-en-semifinales-de-copa-del-rey-f202602-n/).
